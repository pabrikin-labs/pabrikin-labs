Boiler System Diagram

About Boiler System

The boiler system is a critical component in power generation and industrial processes. This diagram illustrates the complete steam generation cycle, from fuel input to steam output, showcasing the intricate flow of materials and energy through various boiler components.

Key Components:

  • Steam Drum: Separates steam from water and maintains water level
  • Superheater: Increases steam temperature above saturation point
  • Economizer: Preheats feedwater using exhaust gases
  • Air Preheater: Improves boiler efficiency by preheating combustion air
  • Electrostatic Precipitator (ESP): Removes particulate matter from flue gases
  • Forced Draft Fan (FD Fan): Supplies combustion air to the boiler
  • Induced Draft Fan (ID Fan): Draws flue gases through the system
  • Boiler Feed Pump (BFP): Supplies high-pressure feedwater to the boiler

Process Flow:

  • Coal enters through the coal feeder and burns in the furnace
  • Heat from combustion boils water in the water walls and steam drum
  • Steam passes through superheater and economizer before exiting
  • Flue gases are cleaned and exhausted through the chimney
  • Feedwater is preheated and recirculated through the system

This diagram represents a typical 75-ton boiler system commonly used in industrial applications, demonstrating the complex interplay between thermal, mechanical, and electrical systems required for efficient steam generation.

About Distribution Network

The distribution network diagram illustrates the complex infrastructure required to deliver essential utilities across an industrial complex. This comprehensive system ensures that steam, electricity, water, and compressed air are efficiently distributed to various processing units within the facility.

Processing Units:

  • General Plant: Central administrative and support facilities
  • Soap Noodle: Soap base manufacturing unit
  • Hydrogenation: Chemical processing for oil hardening
  • Splitting: Separation and purification processes
  • Utility: Central utility generation and distribution
  • FAME (Fatty Acid Methyl Ester): Biodiesel production unit
  • Fractionation: Separation of chemical components
  • Glycerine Refining: Purification of glycerine byproduct
  • Tank Farm: Storage facilities for raw materials and products

Utility Systems:

System Integration:

The diagram demonstrates how utilities are generated centrally and distributed efficiently to minimize energy waste and ensure operational reliability across all plant sections.

Distillation Column Diagram

About Distillation Column

The distillation column diagram shows the separation process of a mixed feed stream into light and heavy fractions. This setup is vital in refining and chemical production, enabling the production of high-purity components through staged vapor-liquid equilibrium.

Key Stages:

  • Reboiler: Provides heat at the bottom to vaporize the liquid mixture
  • Tray/Plate Sections: Provide vapor-liquid contact for separation
  • Condenser: Cools overhead vapor to return part as reflux
  • Reflux Drum: Collects condensed product before recycling or drawing off
  • Bottoms Draw: Extracts the heavy fraction for further processing

Process Flow:

  • Feed enters at an intermediate point then splits into vapor and liquid fractions on trays
  • Light components rise and are condensed at the top, with reflux returned to improve separation
  • Heavy components collect at the bottom and are removed as bottoms product
  • Flow control and reflux ratio govern product purity and energy efficiency

Distillation is one of the most energy-intensive unit operations, and optimizing this column means lower fuel usage and better product yields.
