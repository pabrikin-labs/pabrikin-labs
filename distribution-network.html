About Distribution Network

The distribution network diagram illustrates the complex infrastructure required to deliver essential utilities across an industrial complex. This comprehensive system ensures that steam, electricity, water, and compressed air are efficiently distributed to various processing units within the facility.

Processing Units:

  • General Plant: Central administrative and support facilities
  • Soap Noodle: Soap base manufacturing unit
  • Hydrogenation: Chemical processing for oil hardening
  • Splitting: Separation and purification processes
  • Utility: Central utility generation and distribution
  • FAME (Fatty Acid Methyl Ester): Biodiesel production unit
  • Fractionation: Separation of chemical components
  • Glycerine Refining: Purification of glycerine byproduct
  • Tank Farm: Storage facilities for raw materials and products

Utility Systems:

Steam Distribution

High-pressure steam network supplying heat and power to all processing units. Yellow lines indicate steam flow paths from central boiler to various consumers.

Electrical Distribution

Power distribution system delivering electricity to motors, heaters, and control systems. Red lines show electrical supply routes throughout the facility.

Water Distribution

Raw water supply and wastewater collection system. Blue lines represent water flow for process use, cooling, and effluent management.

Compressed Air

Pneumatic system providing compressed air for instrumentation and process control. Green lines indicate air distribution network.

System Integration:

This distribution network represents a typical integrated chemical processing facility where multiple production units share common utilities. The diagram demonstrates how utilities are generated centrally and distributed efficiently to minimize energy waste and ensure operational reliability across all plant sections.
