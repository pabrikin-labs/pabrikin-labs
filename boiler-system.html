About Boiler System

The boiler system is a critical component in power generation and industrial processes. This diagram illustrates the complete steam generation cycle, from fuel input to steam output, showcasing the intricate flow of materials and energy through various boiler components.

Key Components:

  • Steam Drum: Separates steam from water and maintains water level
  • Superheater: Increases steam temperature above saturation point
  • Economizer: Preheats feedwater using exhaust gases
  • Air Preheater: Improves boiler efficiency by preheating combustion air
  • Electrostatic Precipitator (ESP): Removes particulate matter from flue gases
  • Forced Draft Fan (FD Fan): Supplies combustion air to the boiler
  • Induced Draft Fan (ID Fan): Draws flue gases through the system
  • Boiler Feed Pump (BFP): Supplies high-pressure feedwater to the boiler

Process Flow:

  • Coal enters through the coal feeder and burns in the furnace
  • Heat from combustion boils water in the water walls and steam drum
  • Steam passes through superheater and economizer before exiting
  • Flue gases are cleaned and exhausted through the chimney
  • Feedwater is preheated and recirculated through the system

This diagram represents a typical 75-ton boiler system commonly used in industrial applications, demonstrating the complex interplay between thermal, mechanical, and electrical systems required for efficient steam generation.
